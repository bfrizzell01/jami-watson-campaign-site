Community

Everyone deserves to feel like they belong.

A strong community isn’t built by government alone—it’s built by neighbours, volunteers, local businesses, community organizations, and people who choose to show up for one another. I believe Council’s role is to create the conditions where those connections can grow.

Through my work at Costco, volunteering with Rotary, serving on the Parks, Recreation and Culture Advisory Committee, and meeting residents across Langley, I’ve seen what happens when people feel connected to their community. They volunteer. They support local businesses. They care for parks and public spaces. They look out for their neighbours.

When community initiatives come before Council, I’ll ask:

  • Does this help people connect?
  • Does it strengthen local organizations and volunteers?
  • Does it create welcoming spaces where people of all ages and abilities feel they belong?
  • Will this strengthen our community for years to come?

I believe one of the greatest responsibilities of local government is helping create a Township where people don’t just live, it's a place they call home.
